Quick answer

The Clarisonic Mia 2 remains the top pick for most people, offering a thorough yet gentle cleanse that dermatologists and beauty editors have trusted for years.

The FOREO Luna 2 Pro is ideal if you want a silicone option that’s gentler and more hygienic, while the no‑frills Olay Pro‑X is the smart choice for anyone watching their spending, and the NuFACE Trinity PRO brings microcurrent toning to the table if anti‑aging is your priority.

The right facial cleansing device can transform your daily routine from a quick splash into a spa‑worthy ritual that preps your skin to absorb serums and moisturizers more effectively. But with so many spinning, sonic, and vibrating brushes on the market, it’s easy to get lost in the noise. When shopping, focus on bristle type (nylon vs. silicone), speed settings, how it handles different skin types, and how easy it is to keep clean. A device that forces you to replace heads often or that irritates sensitive skin isn’t worth the counter space, even if it comes with glowing reviews.

Best for specific needs

Best for deep pore cleaning

If your main concern is thoroughly removing blackheads, makeup residue, and daily grime, the Clarisonic Mia 2’s oscillating brush head is the most proven option. Its two speeds let you go a bit more aggressive on oily zones, but be careful with pressure. This is the device that countless dermatologists have recommended for that just‑cleaned, polished finish. Our pick: Clarisonic Mia 2.

Best for sensitive and reactive skin

The FOREO Luna 2 Pro is the gentlest choice here. Its silicone bristles are soft against the skin, and the T‑Sonic vibrations lift impurities without the abrasive friction of rotating brushes. There’s no brush head to replace, so it stays free of bacteria, a huge plus for anyone prone to breakouts or irritation. Our pick: FOREO Luna 2 Pro.

Best on a strict budget

When money is tight, the Olay Pro‑X gives you a spinning brush that works well for the price. It won’t knock your socks off with fancy technology, but it does the basics, cleansing off makeup and excess oil, without breaking the bank. Pair it with a gentle cleanser, and you’ve got a solid starter routine for under a few dollars more. Our pick: Olay Pro-X.

Our verdict

For most people, the Clarisonic Mia 2 is still the cleanser to beat: it’s effective, widely recommended, and versatile enough for daily use. If you have sensitive skin or simply hate replacing brush heads, the FOREO Luna 2 Pro is well worth the upgrade. And if your budget won’t stretch that far, the Olay Pro‑X proves you don’t have to spend a lot to get a noticeably cleaner face. The NuFACE Trinity PRO and Vivid Lite serve narrower audiences, anti‑aging enthusiasts and ultra‑budget silicone fans, respectively, but both have their place. Whichever you choose, stick with a consistent routine, and your skin will thank you.
